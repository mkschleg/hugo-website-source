## Introduction {#introduction}

Philosophy lives at the edges of both science and theology. Where all definite knowledge belongs to science, dogma belongs to theology. Although I would argue that there is dogmatic science as well, but maybe we would prefer to represent this as a pseudo.

Philosophy sits in-between grappling with the questions science is unable to answer, while not providing dogmatic perspectives or confident answers provided by the theologians.


### Timeline {#timeline}

<!--list-separator-->

-  6th Century B.C.E

    Philosophy as a distinct discipline began in greece in the 6th centure B.C.E.

<!--list-separator-->

-  11th-14th A.D.

    Submerged by theology as Christianity rose and Rome fell. This era was dominated by the catholic church.

<!--list-separator-->

-  17th - Now

    Dominated by science.

### Definitions: {#definitions}

<!--list-separator-->

-  science

    All things containing definite knowledge. This is the field of scientific inquiry, in looking for evidence based explanations of natural phenomenon.

<!--list-separator-->

-  theology

    This is the polar opposite of science where explanations for phenomenon are given concrete answers without evidence, often inducing _dogma_.
