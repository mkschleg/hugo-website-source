There are several lines of research focused on reinforcement learning in the brain. Most of these are looking at the roll of [Dopamine]({{< relref "dopamine.md" >}}) in learning. (<a href="#citeproc_bib_item_2">Niv 2009</a>) is a nice synopsis of the major lines of work and future directions for uncovering the roll of the [Reinforcement Learning]({{< relref "reinforcement_learning.md" >}}) in explaining the brain.


## Reward Prediction-Error Hypothesis of Dopamine {#reward-prediction-error-hypothesis-of-dopamine}

> According to the reward-prediction error hypothesis of dopa- mine (RPEH), the phasic activity of dopaminergic neurons in spe- cific regions in the midbrain signals a discrepancy between the predicted and currently experienced reward of a particular event. (<a href="#citeproc_bib_item_1">Colombo 2014</a>)
