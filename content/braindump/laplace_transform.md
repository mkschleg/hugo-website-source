The Laplace Transform is a particular type of [Integral Transform]({{<relref "integral_transform.md#" >}}).

Let \\(f(t)\\) be given for \\(t\ge0\\)

\\[
\mathcal{L}\\{ f(t) \\} = F(s) = \int\_{0}^{\infty} e^{-st} f(t) dt
\\]

The Laplace Transform $F(s) for \\(s>\alpha\\) exists if

1.  \\(f\\) is a piecewise continuous function on the interval \\(0\leq t \leq A \in \reals^{+}\\)
2.  \\(|f(t)| \leq k e^{\alpha t}\\) when \\(t \geq \mu\\). \\(k, \mu \in \reals^{+}\\), and \\(\alpha \in \reals\\).
